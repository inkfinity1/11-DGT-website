Cantonses (各州)

Cantonese cuisine, also known as Yue cuisine, is one of the Eight Culinary Traditions of Chinese cuisine. It has a rich history, deeply rooted in the Lingnan region of Guangdong Province. Renowned for its fresh ingredients, light seasoning, and emphasis on natural flavors, it has become a global ambassador for Chinese culinary art.

Fresh seafood, seasonal vegetables, and a wide variety of meats characterize this cuisine. The cooking techniques are diverse, with steaming, stir-frying, and roasting as favorites. The dishes are often delicate in flavor, avoiding heavy spices to let the ingredients shine.

Some iconic dishes include:

Dim Sum (点心): A collection of bite-sized delights, such as shrimp dumplings and barbecue pork buns.

Sweet and savory roasted pork (叉烧): This dish features tender, roasted pork with its signature reddish glaze.

Steamed Fish (清蒸鱼): A simple yet flavorful dish highlighting the freshness of the seafood.
