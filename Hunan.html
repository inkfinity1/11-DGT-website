Hunan (湖南)

Hunan cuisine, also known as Xiang cuisine (湘菜), is a fiery and flavor-packed tradition from China's Hunan Province. Famous for its bold use of chili peppers, garlic, and shallots, it creates dishes with a tantalizing heat and depth. Unlike Sichuan cuisine, which often employs numbing Sichuan peppercorns, Hunan cooking focuses purely on spiciness, resulting in an intense, rich taste. Steaming, stewing, and stir-frying are common techniques that preserve the natural flavors of the ingredients.

Some iconic dishes include:

Hunan Stir-Fried Spicy Beef (湖南小炒黄牛肉): Thinly sliced beef stir-fried with garlic, chili, and a touch of cumin.

Dong'an Chicken (东安鸡): A tangy, mildly spicy chicken dish flavored with vinegar, ginger, and chili oil.

Steamed Fish Head with Chopped Chili (剁椒鱼头): A spicy, aromatic dish highlighting the fresh, tender fish meat.
